## What to research — the dimension checklist

For every competitor, capture (with specifics — numbers, quotes, not adjectives):

- [ ] **Positioning** — headline, niche, the one-line value prop, tone
- [ ] **Services & packages** — what's offered, are there named tiers/bundles, advisory vs compliance split
- [ ] **Pricing** — shown or hidden? model (fixed monthly / quote / from-£X)? any numbers leaked
- [ ] **Proof & trust** — Google/Trustpilot review COUNT + rating, testimonials, case studies (with metrics?), badges (ICAEW/CIOT/ACCA/Xero tier), awards (named?)
- [ ] **Conversion mechanics** — primary CTA, booking flow, lead magnets (guides/calculators/benchmarks), number of forms, friction
- [ ] **Content & SEO** — landing-page depth (word count), blog cadence & topics, target keywords, listicles, FAQ + schema, free tools
- [ ] **Social & authority** — LinkedIn presence/followers, YouTube/video, newsletter, podcasts, partnerships
- [ ] **Site/UX** — design quality, speed feel, mobile, anything notably good or broken
- [ ] **Signature tactic** — the one thing they do better than the rest
- [ ] **Weakness / gap** — where they're beatable

## Method

Five parallel research agents, two competitors each, reporting against the
checklist above. Findings synthesised below into (1) a comparison matrix and
(2) a "copy / beat / ignore" action list for Simon's site.

## The patterns (what they're all doing)

1. **Reviews are the moat — and they're the thing Simon most lacks.** The two firms ranking **#1 (A&C, Agile) win largely on review volume + freshness** (100+ / 130, both 5.0). Crunch (1,551), Smooth (103), COPA/Rise (~45–50) all surface counts. The ones with *no* public reviews — Wow, Sidekick, David Howard, Foxsmith — are exposed. **Simon has zero. This is the #1 build item.**
2. **Two proven routes to #1, both visible here:** (a) **aged domain + multi-office local SEO** (David Howard, A&C) — *can't be copied fast*; (b) **deep niche page + review velocity + programmatic vertical pages** (Agile, Sidekick) — **Agile did this from a 2022 domain, so it's achievable.** Plus Rise's **self-ranking listicle** play.
3. **Pricing splits the market.** Transparent (Agile calculator, COPA £75, Crunch tiers) converts cold traffic; the premium majority hides it. Agile's calculator is its single biggest weapon.
4. **The leaders have a *tool or data asset*, not just a brochure** — Sidekick's scorecard, Wow's BenchPress. A diagnostic/benchmark captures + qualifies leads. Simon has none.
5. **Nobody proves with numbers.** Case studies across the entire field are qualitative ("exceptional growth"); the *only* hard figures found were Sidekick's "saved £10k." **One quantified case study would out-prove all ten.**
6. **The exit angle is wide open.** Only Wow (M&A service, gated to £1m+) and Rise (EMI/valuations) touch it; **nobody leads with "plan tax around your exit."**
7. **Credentials: Simon is at the top of the field.** Only Foxsmith (private-client, not an agency rival) matches CTA+ICAEW. **No agency specialist has CTA · ACA · ex-PwC.**

## What Simon should do — copy / beat / ignore

### ✅ COPY (proven to win — build these)
- **A visible review wall.** Set up Google Business Profile + harvest reviews now; target 50+ to match the #1 rankers. *(feeds discovery checklist #6)* — highest-leverage single move.
- **A deep agency landing page (1,500–2,500 words) + a few programmatic sub-niche pages** (e.g. SEO/PPC/social agencies) — the SEO layer the current beautiful-but-thin site lacks. This is the Agile/Sidekick route to #1. *(→ Gate 2 sitemap)*
- **A lead-magnet tool**, not a brochure — an "Agency Tax & Profit Health Check" scorecard or a "What's your agency worth at exit?" calculator (Sidekick/Wow model; ties to the exit angle).
- **"Book a discovery call" flow** — wire the Google calendar link (already have it).
- **Personal-brand authority** via video/short content — Simon already speaks publicly; Smooth proves it works, and *no agency specialist* is doing it.

### ⚔️ BEAT (their universal weaknesses → Simon's edge)
- **Quantified case studies** — nobody has them; even one "saved £X / structured Y" beats the field. *(discovery checklist #9 — chase this)*
- **Lead with credentials** — CTA · ACA · ex-PwC; no agency rival can match it.
- **Lead with the exit** — own "plan tax around your exit, not your year-end" for founder-led agencies, including the **sub-£1m band Wow rejects**.
- **Niche purity** — be agency-only, not a generalist with one landing page (COPA, A&C, David Howard, Smooth all fail here).
- **Reduce pricing opacity** — without a full price list (Simon's settled call), a "how our pricing works / typical ranges" explainer would borrow Agile's conversion edge while keeping his positioning. *(tension to resolve with Simon)*

### 🚫 IGNORE (wrong fit / can't fake)
- **Foxsmith's referral-only, no-reviews model** — wrong market; keep only as the *visual* design reference (navy + cream, badge wall).
- **Crunch's low-price self-serve** — race to the bottom, wrong positioning.
- **David Howard's heritage + 11-office local SEO** — can't fake 50 years or 11 offices as a new solo brand.
- **Wow's £1m+ gate** — Simon should welcome the £500k–£5m band, sub-£1m included.

### The open lane (one sentence)
> A genuine **agency-only** adviser with **top credentials (CTA·ACA·ex-PwC)** who **leads with the exit**, **proves it with real numbers**, and is **easy to find (deep SEO + a visible review wall) and easy to book** — the corner no one in this set occupies at once.
