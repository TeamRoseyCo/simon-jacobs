## Part A — Discovery: what we still need from Simon

We never got an intake form back, so this is the list to put in front of him. Grouped
by how much it blocks the build. The ICP, copy, and bio are done — these are the
**gaps the existing docs don't answer**, several of them confirmed as competitor
table-stakes by the teardown below.

### 🔴 Blocks conversion — get these first
1. **Booking link.** Every rival's main CTA is "Book a call." We're still on a
   `mailto:`. Does he have (or will he set up) a Calendly / Cal.com link for the
   15-min discovery call? This is the single biggest conversion fix.
2. **Where do leads go?** To wire the three forms (contact, consult, newsletter)
   at Gate 5 we need a destination: which inbox, and is there a CRM/mailing tool
   (Mailchimp, HubSpot, a spreadsheet)?
3. **Accreditation marks.** Every competitor shows badges (ICAEW, CIOT, Xero,
   ACCA). We only have the *words* "CTA · ACA · Ex-PwC." Can he supply / approve
   use of: CIOT (CTA) mark, ICAEW (ACA) mark, and any PwC-alumni mark? Highest-
   value missing trust element.

### 🟠 Sharpens positioning — needed to lock copy
4. **Niche: hard or soft?** Is "UK marketing/digital agencies" a *hard* niche, or
   just the strongest example? Two of his three testimonials are a **dentist** and
   a **landlord** — that contradicts the single-agency ICP. Decide: do we drop the
   non-agency quotes for sharper focus, or keep them and soften the niche claim?
5. **Brand name.** The site shows the firm as **"SRJ International"** but the email
   is **simon@jacobs-taxes.com** and the project is "Simon Jacobs." What's the
   canonical brand — personal name, SRJ, or jacobs-taxes? Logo file?
6. **Reviews / rating.** Smooth leads with "4.8 from 103 Google reviews," Crunch
   with "Trustpilot 5.0, 64,000 clients." Does Simon have a Google Business profile
   or Trustpilot we can surface? Even a modest real rating beats none.

### 🟡 Strengthens proof — nice to have for launch
7. **Agency-specific testimonials.** Current quotes are strong but mixed-sector.
   Can he get 1–2 lines from actual *agency* founders (first name + "digital agency"
   is enough)?
8. **Xero / QuickBooks partner status** (and tier). "He runs the software you
   already use" is a quiet trust win the rivals all use.
9. **Quantified outcome / mini case study.** Rivals are *weak* here — vague
   "exceptional growth." One real "saved £X / structured Y" line would out-prove
   all of them.
10. **Awards / memberships** worth displaying, if any.

### 🟢 Brand & assets — already partly answered, confirm
11. Colour scheme (blue + teal + seafoam) and the italic Playfair emphasis — does
    he like them? (Carried over from `later-improvements.md`.)
12. Tone sign-off: is the plain-English, slightly cheeky line ("Stop using Claude
    for taxes") on-brand for him?
13. Prague photos / headshot usage rights.

> **Settled, don't re-ask:** No price list on the site (his call — generic firms do
> that). The teardown *confirms* this: **none** of the five rivals publish full
> prices. He's in line with the market.

## Part B — Competitor teardown

Four same-industry rivals + one broad. Reviewed June 2026.

### Same industry

#### 1. The Wow Company — `thewowcompany.com` *(the brand to beat)*
- **What they say:** "Accountants Businesses Love." Explicitly targets **agencies,
  consultancies and tech businesses at £1m+ turnover.** "We love working with
  agencies." Warm, "we've got your back" tone over technical credentials.
- **How they sell:** "Book a call at a time that suits you." No pricing. Xero
  **Platinum** Partner badge, "award-winning team," FCA-regulated, success-stories
  section, benchmarking ("latest benchmarks, trends").
- **Where they're weak:** Claims awards/benchmarking but shows **no actual sample
  reports, testimonials, or client logos**. "Make your life easier and your
  business better" is generic. Says it's "not like other accountants" without
  explaining why.
- **vs. Simon:** Closest positioning rival and the strongest brand. They own the
  warm-generalist agency space at £1m+. **Simon's wedges:** narrower (founder-led,
  exit-focused), more personal (one named CTA+ACA+ex-PwC adviser vs. a team), and
  he names the *exit/valuation* outcome they don't.

#### 2. Sidekick Accounting — `sidekickaccounting.co.uk` *(closest direct rival)*
- **What they say:** "Accountants for Digital Marketing Agencies." Problem/solution:
  "tired of a general accountant who isn't proactive." Names agency pains (ad spend,
  international clients, multiple revenue streams). Empathetic, not casual.
- **How they sell:** "Schedule a Call" (7+ times). No pricing. ICAEW logo, 10
  agency sub-sectors, a "client wins" page, newsletter.
- **Where they're weak:** A visible **copy-paste error** ("look no further than
  Capture Accounting" — wrong brand) on the agency page. **No testimonials, case
  studies, or quantified results** on that page despite selling "insights." Assumes
  every agency wants fractional-CFO support.
- **vs. Simon:** Same target, same problem framing — but sloppy and unproven. Simon
  wins on credentials (CTA+ACA+ex-PwC vs. ICAEW only), polish, and the exit angle.

#### 3. Foxsmith (Fox Smith Associates) — `foxsmith.co.uk` *(Simon's font reference)*
- **What they say:** "Personalised Service, Expert Tax Solutions." High-end tax for
  business owners, **non-doms, HNW families, offshore trusts** — *not* agency-
  focused. Professional, reassuring, "safe hands."
- **How they sell:** "Get In Touch" contact form (no booking system). No pricing.
  Strong badge wall: **ICAEW, ACCA, CIOT, STEP.** Names "several large offshore
  Trust Companies." Regatta sponsorship.
- **Where they're weak:** **No testimonials or case studies.** Badges are standard
  and don't differentiate. Vague "peace of mind," no quantified outcomes, single
  contact CTA, no resources/nurture.
- **vs. Simon:** Different niche (private-client/trusts, not agencies). Useful only
  as the **visual reference** Simon likes (warm, classic). Not a sales threat.

#### 4. Smooth Accounting — `smoothaccounting.co.uk` *(Allan's modern reference)*
- **What they say:** "Multi-award-winning Chartered Accountants" for SMEs. Modern,
  anti-stuffy, plain-English ("won't use tricky jargon"), **4-hour response**
  promise. Broad target incl. influencers/creators, landlords, AI companies.
- **How they sell:** "Book A Call With Us" everywhere; free discovery calls. No
  pricing. Strong proof: **6 named case studies, ICAEW + Xero Gold + Cyber
  Essentials, "4.8 from 103 Google reviews,"** tree-planting cause, named clients.
- **Where they're weak:** "Multi-award-winning" with **no award named.** No metrics
  in case studies ("exceptional growth" = 5% or 500%?). Influencer focus feels
  scattershot. Casual tone risks the gravitas a serious founder may want.
- **vs. Simon:** Best *proof stack* of the set — this is the bar for our trust
  signals (reviews + badges + named clients). But they're a broad generalist;
  Simon's focus and exit angle are sharper. **Their reviews are what we most lack.**

### Broad comparison

#### 5. Crunch — `crunch.co.uk` *(the generic online accountant)*
- **What they say:** "Feel in control of every number, every day." Online
  accountants + software for **sole traders, ltd cos, e-commerce.** "One fixed fee
  as you grow." Friendly, simple.
- **How they sell:** Instant-quote generator **and** book-a-call (dual path). Tiered
  software pricing shown; service pricing dynamic. Huge proof: **Trustpilot 5.0,
  1,500+ reviews, 64,000 clients since 2009, ACCA/AAT/CIOT/ICAEW/IFA badges, multiple
  awards, HMRC-recognised.**
- **Where they're weak (for our ICP):** Compliance-first and **reactive** — "file it
  with HMRC, no surprises," not forward planning. **No proactive tax/exit strategy,
  no named dedicated adviser, all-or-nothing service bundles, hidden add-on pricing.**
  Built for stable small businesses, not a £500k–£5m founder scaling toward a sale.
- **vs. Simon:** This is the "cheap default" a founder might already use. Simon is
  the **opposite end** — proactive, personal, exit-minded advisory. The contrast is
  the pitch: *"Crunch files your numbers. Simon plans around them."*

### The #1-ranked rivals (Hazem's finds — weak sites that still rank top)

These four rank **#1 on Google** for agency-accountant searches despite being
visually weak. That contradiction is the lesson: **Google ranks SEO, not design.**

| Firm | URL | Why it ranks |
|---|---|---|
| **A&C Chartered Accountants** | ac-accounts.co.uk | 100+ Google reviews (5★), 30yr domain, Manchester local SEO, 40+ internal links, one landing page per sector, ~1,200–1,500 words. Real case study (Searchmode) + Xero Platinum + ICAEW badges. |
| **David Howard** | davidhoward.co.uk | "Trusted since 1973" (50yr), **11 office locations** = local SEO across the SE, ~1,800–2,000 words, 3 named testimonials, keyword "marketing agencies" 8+ times. |
| **Agile Accountants** | agileaccountants.co.uk | **5.0 from 130 Google reviews**, ~2,200–2,500 words, instant-quote tool, jump-to-section anchors, agency-specific long-tail ("Digital VAT", "IR35", "campaign-level revenue"), Birmingham local. |
| **Rise Accounting** | riseaccounting.co.uk/blog | Not a service page — a **listicle**: "Best Accountants for Agencies UK 2026," ~2,500 words, comparison table + FAQ, ranks 6 firms and slots itself #1. A content/lead-gen play. |

**What they ALL have that our site does NOT (this is the real gap):**
1. **Long-form, sector-specific landing-page copy** (1,400–2,500 words). Our pages
   are short and beautiful — great for conversion, **invisible to Google.**
2. **Volume of Google reviews** (100–130). We have 3 testimonials, no rating.
3. **Location signals + domain age.** New `.vercel.app` domain, no Google Business
   profile, no local targeting = a standing start.
4. **Internal linking depth** and an FAQ block (for "People Also Ask" snippets).

> **Strategic takeaway:** the current site is built to *convert* a visitor but not
> to *get found*. To beat these #1 sites we don't need a prettier site — we need an
> **SEO layer**: a deep agency landing page, an FAQ with schema, Google reviews, a
> real domain + Google Business profile, and ideally our own comparison/blog content.

### What the teardown tells us (patterns)

1. **No one publishes prices** → Simon's no-price-list call is correct, not a gap.
2. **Everyone's main CTA is "Book a call"** → our `mailto:` is behind the market.
   Fix #1.
3. **Everyone shows accreditation badges** → our words-only credentials are a real
   gap. Fix #3.
4. **Reviews/ratings are the strongest proof in the set** (Smooth's 103 Google
   reviews, Crunch's Trustpilot) → if Simon has any rating, surface it.
5. **The universal weakness is vagueness** — "award-winning," "exceptional growth,"
   no numbers, no named adviser, no exit/valuation story. **Simon's white space:**
   a named, credentialed, agency-specialist adviser who leads with *exit value*, in
   plain English, with one or two *quantified* proofs. Nobody else owns that corner.

## Gate 1 close — the 3 sentences (for Simon to confirm)

1. **Goal:** Get founder-led UK agencies to book a discovery call, by promising to
   help them keep more profit *and* build a business that's actually worth selling.
2. **Customer:** The founder of a UK digital/marketing agency turning over £500k–£5m,
   late-20s to 30s, who decides fast, is cost-sensitive, and arrives from social or a
   referral.
3. **Angle:** The only agency-specialist who plans tax around your *exit*, not just
   your year-end — Big-Four-trained (CTA · ACA · ex-PwC) advice in plain English.
