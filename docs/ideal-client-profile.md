# Ideal Client Profile (ICP)

> Transcribed from the "Ideal client" canvas (mel). This is the single source of
> truth for who the site speaks to. Copy, structure, and CTAs across the site
> should map back to the pains, goals, and attributes below.

## The profile

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Industry** | Digital marketing agency, focusing on AI. Based in the UK. |
| **Geographic reach** | UK predominantly; serves clients worldwide. |
| **Company size** | 1–20 employees. |
| **Annual turnover** | £500k – £5m, with ~30% profit before tax (≈ £150,000 – £1,500,000 PBT). |
| **Buying process** | Finds accountants/tax advisers via social media or referrals, then jumps on a discovery call. |
| **Decision maker** | The CEO — a young founder, late 20s / early 30s. Sole decision maker. |
| **Pain points** | High taxes (corporation, VAT, personal); high costs/expenses; staying competitive in a saturated market. |
| **Business goals** | Scale the company to a 5–10 year exit at a significant valuation. |
| **Attributes** | Decides fast; cut-throat about cutting costs that run too high; tech-savvy and forward-thinking. |

## What this changes about the site

**1. Sharpen the target.** Current copy says "UK marketing agencies" broadly. The
ICP is narrower and more specific: **UK digital/AI marketing agencies, £500k–£5m
turnover, founder-led.** Tightening the language ("growing UK digital agencies"
rather than generic "agencies") makes the founder feel the site is for *them*.

**2. Lead with the exit, not just the year-end.** Their #1 goal is to **scale and
exit in 5–10 years at a strong valuation.** That reframes the value proposition:
this isn't only "keep more of what you earn" — it's "build a more valuable,
sellable business." Tax planning + clean books + profit extraction all ladder up
to a higher-multiple exit. This should appear in the hero and About/positioning.

**3. Speak to the three real pains.**
- High taxes — corporation, VAT, **and personal** (most accountants ignore the
  personal/director side; the site already touches profit extraction — lean in).
- High costs in a saturated market — they're cost-sensitive and "cut-throat" about
  spend, so the messaging must justify ROI fast and avoid sounding like fluff.
- Competitiveness — frame advice as a margin/edge, not overhead.

**4. Match the buyer's tempo.** They **decide fast, are tech-savvy, and buy off a
discovery call** sourced from social/referrals. Implications:
- Primary CTA should be **"Book a discovery call,"** not "Email Simon" — match the
  exact step in their buying process.
- Keep copy punchy and skimmable; a fast decider won't read dense paragraphs.
- Make the path to the call short and obvious on every page.
- Social-proof / credibility (CTA·CA, ex-PwC) matters because they arrive cold
  from social and need a fast trust signal.

**5. Address the sole founder directly.** One decision maker, no committee — write
in second person ("you," "your agency"), not "businesses."

## Voice of customer (research notes)

> **Method note / limitation:** The richest customer-voice sources — Reddit, X,
> Threads, and Amazon/Google reviews — are **not accessible** to the research tool
> (Reddit is hard-blocked; X/Threads/reviews are login-gated or JS-rendered). The
> phrases below are **representative**, synthesized from the ICP plus publicly
> indexed UK agency-accounting and agency-valuation sources — not verbatim scrapes.
> To get real verbatim quotes, mine r/agency, r/digital_marketing, r/AskUK_Money,
> and agency-owner X/Threads manually and drop them in here.

**Real behavioural insights from accessible sources (usable as proof/positioning):**
- Founders often treat their own pay as a *personal withdrawal, not a business
  expense*, causing a **boom-and-bust pay cycle** — too little when cash is tight,
  too much after a big client pays. (Sidekick / 1st Formations)
- **Owner-dependency lowers the exit multiple**: if the founder is lead salesperson,
  main client relationship, and head of delivery, buyers apply lower multiples and
  heavier earnouts. (FE International / Breakwater M&A)
- The **best exits are planned 12–24 months ahead** — clean financials, diversified
  clients, reduced owner-dependency. Prepared agencies exit at ~1.5–2× the multiple
  of unprepared ones on the same earnings. (FE International)

**Representative phrase bank (the founder's own words — use sparingly in copy):**
- "I'm working flat out and the tax bill still eats most of it."
- "I never know how much to actually pay myself."
- "My accountant only shows up at year-end, when it's too late to change anything."
- "I want to build something I can sell, not just a job that pays me."
- "Every cost is under the microscope — I can't afford waste."
- "I'm good at winning clients. The money side is a black box."
